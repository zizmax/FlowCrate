Flow Crate fetches the newest post from the Flow State newsletter and plays the picks on your Spotify — or straight to your Sonos speakers with "Hey Siri, play Flow Crate."

Terminal
$ pipx install git+https://github.com/zizmax/FlowCrate.git
$ flowcrate

Requires Python 3.10+ and Spotify Premium. Runs entirely on your computer.

How it works

Up and running in minutes.

01

Install & run

One command with pipx, then type flowcrate. The dashboard opens in your browser at localhost:8765.

02

Connect Spotify

Settings walks you through creating your own free Spotify developer app. Your keys never leave your machine — no backend, no middleman.

03

Press play

The newest Flow State picks, matched on Spotify and ready to queue. Sonos and Siri support are optional — one click to set up.

Features

Built to stay out of your way.

Local-first

No accounts, no cloud, no telemetry. Your Spotify credentials live in ~/.flowcrate on your machine — nowhere else.

Sonos, direct

Speaks to your speakers over your local network using SoCo — no cloud round-trip, no Sonos account required beyond your existing setup.

Hey Siri

A one-tap downloadable Shortcut wires up voice control. Say "Hey Siri, play Flow Crate" and Siri confirms what's now playing on your Sonos.

Rate-limit friendly

Smart local caching means Spotify is only queried once per track, ever. Repeat listens are instant; your API quota stays untouched.

Flow Crate is an unofficial personal project, unaffiliated with Flow State, Spotify, Sonos, or Apple. It never redistributes newsletter content — subscribe at flowstate.fm and support the artists.
